01 · Company & voice
About Climoo

Brazilian climate-tech, built for the framework world

Climoo is a Brazilian technology + consulting company. Its SaaS platform helps mid-market and enterprise companies measure, plan and track carbon emissions in compliance with GHG Protocol, CDP, TCFD, IFRS and SBTi.

What Climoo sells

An integrated platform covering emissions inventory (scopes 1/2/3), ESG indicator management, decarbonization planning, SBTi target submission, and TCFD-aligned climate risk analysis.

200+ companies served · 15 countries. Named clients include Eurofarma, Movida, Mills, RD Saúde and Special Dog.

Voice in three words

Expert. Talks to people who already know the acronyms. Doesn't explain SBTi or CSRD.

Pragmatic. Lives in the gap between consulting expertise and auditable software.

Composed. Never preachy. No climate doom, no exclamation points, no emoji.

Phrases that sound like Climoo
"Create and manage your decarbonization strategy."
"More clarity, less effort."
"Your emissions management doesn't have to be like this."
"This isn't lack of climate commitment. It's lack of a tool made for your business."
"The first 30 days are on us. No credit card, no commitment."

08 · Brand motifs
Brand

The blob & the rings

Two motifs do most of the brand-recognition work. Use one or the other — never both — when establishing a hero.

Hero blob — the top-left ellipse
Create and manage your decarbonization strategy
Integrated solution for emissions inventories, SBTi targets and decarbonization plans.
Schedule a demo

A 90vw × 185vh ellipse positioned top-left of the hero, bleeding off both edges. Pure #210856 fill, no gradient. On mobile it becomes a flat rectangle covering the full viewport.

Concentric gradient rings — used on solution detail pages
Climate risk analysis aligned with TCFD
Identify and assess climate-related risks and opportunities in line with the recommendations of the TCFD.

Four nested rings drawn with a padding-box / border-box gradient-border trick so the stroke itself fades from cyan → violet → purple. Built only at scale on solution pages.

Usage rules
stroke-width
1.5–2px. Never filled-solid (except the WhatsApp icon in the footer).
size
16–24px for inline icons; 36px for feature-card hero icons.
color
Default inherits text color. Feature cards override to #7C3AED bright violet.
accessibility
Always aria-hidden="true" if the icon sits next to a text label.
emoji
Never. The Climoo voice does not use emoji in UI or marketing.

11 · Voice — do & don't
Voice

Concrete moves, & what to skip

Climoo writes plainly around technical acronyms. Sentence-case everything, address the customer directly, write as "we", problem-then-solution.

Do
  • Sentence-case headlines: "Create and manage your decarbonization strategy"
  • Address the reader as you; the company as we
  • Use acronyms (SBTi, GHG, TCFD, IFRS, CSRD) without explaining them
  • Frame a problem before pitching the solution
  • Keep card descriptions under ~25 words
  • Quote real numbers (200+ companies, 15 countries, 30-day free trial)
Don't
  • No exclamation points outside FAQ / support reply contexts
  • No emoji in UI, headlines, or body copy
  • No climate-doom or urgency framing ("the planet is dying")
  • No tech-bro language ("disrupt", "10x", "revolutionize")
  • No "AI-powered" framing — Climoo leads on expertise + auditability
  • No Title Case in headlines (reserved for product / proper nouns)
Phrase bank — quote freely
"Create and manage your decarbonization strategy."
"Centralize your emissions data — eliminate scattered spreadsheets."
"ESG reports without rework."
"More clarity, less effort."
"Built to work from day one."
"This isn't lack of climate commitment. It's lack of a tool made for your business."
Pattern: problem → solution

Climoo's marketing site uses an explicit two-beat: first call out a problem the reader recognizes ("fragmented data", "inconsistent reports", "slow manual collection"), then position the platform as the fix. Reach for this structure when writing new copy — it's load-bearing.
